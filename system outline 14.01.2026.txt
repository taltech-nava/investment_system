### 1. Full system outline – the desired end state

Think of the final system as your **personal investment intelligence platform**, with four main layers and several core modules.

---

#### 1.1. Core objectives

- **Centralize knowledge:** all theses, trades, analyses, external opinions, and files are structured and searchable.
- **Continuously monitor:** accounts, prices, fundamentals, news, and risk.
- **Compare expectations vs outcomes:** systematically track forecast accuracy and thesis performance.
- **Learn and adapt:** refine rules, heuristics, and models based on your own history.
- **Generate actions:** alerts, reviews, and prompts that actually change decisions, not just decorate them.

---

#### 1.2. System architecture overview

- **Data layer:** storage, schema, and file organization.
- **Logic layer:** analytics, rules, models.
- **Interface layer:** how you see and interact with the system.
- **Automation layer:** scheduled tasks, alerts, and reviews.

Each layer can be simple at first and more sophisticated later.

---

#### 1.3. Data layer – what the system knows

**Entities:**

- **Instrument**
  - Ticker, ISIN, asset class (stock, ETF, crypto, loan, etc.), sector, geography, risk bucket, tags.

- **Account & position**
  - Broker/platform, instrument, size, entry/exit dates and prices, fees, realized/unrealized P&L, currency.

- **Thesis**
  - Instruments involved.
  - Direction (long/short/avoid).
  - Expected return range and time horizon.
  - Key drivers and catalysts.
  - Key risks.
  - Valuation logic.
  - Conviction score and confidence in forecast.
  - Status (planned, active, closed, invalidated).
  - Links to supporting documents (notes, spreadsheets, PDFs).

- **Expectation snapshot**
  - For each thesis at creation:
    - Expected return distribution (e.g. base case, bull, bear).
    - Expected volatility / drawdown tolerance.
    - Expected path (smooth, choppy, binary).
    - Explicit “what would make me change my mind”.

- **Outcome snapshot**
  - At review points:
    - Actual return.
    - Time elapsed.
    - Max drawdown.
    - Key events (earnings, macro, company-specific).
    - Whether thesis played out, failed, or is still uncertain.

- **External opinion**
  - Source, author, date.
  - Instrument/sector.
  - Rating/target, horizon.
  - Summary of arguments.
  - Quality metrics (track record, transparency, conflicts).
  - Your subjective trust score.

- **Event / alert**
  - Trigger type (price move, news, fundamental change, time-based review).
  - Instrument/portfolio context.
  - Your response (acted/ignored).
  - Outcome (helpful, neutral, harmful).

- **Behavioral log**
  - Deviations from plan (panic sell, FOMO buy, ignored stop, etc.).
  - Emotional notes if you want them (optional but powerful).

All of this can live in a **local database (e.g. SQLite)** plus a structured file system for documents.

---

#### 1.4. Logic layer – what the system does

**A. Instrument & portfolio analytics**

- **Technical:**
  - Price history, moving averages, volatility, drawdowns, trend regime.
  - Simple signals (breakouts, mean reversion, volatility spikes).

- **Fundamental:**
  - Key ratios and trends (P/E, P/B, EV/EBITDA, margins, growth, leverage).
  - Relative valuation vs sector and own history.
  - Quality and cyclicality indicators.

- **Portfolio:**
  - Exposure by sector, geography, asset class, factor.
  - Concentration, diversification, correlation.
  - Risk metrics (volatility, drawdown, scenario stress tests).

**B. Analyst/meta-analysis module**

- Aggregate external opinions by instrument, sector, and source.
- Track:
  - Hit rates and calibration of each source.
  - Biases (perma-bull, perma-bear, late to trend).
  - Agreement vs dispersion across analysts.
- Provide:
  - Consensus view.
  - Quality-weighted view.
  - “Outlier” opinions worth attention.

**C. Expectation vs outcome engine**

- For each thesis:
  - Compare expected vs actual return at multiple checkpoints.
  - Compare expected vs actual time to outcome.
  - Compare expected vs actual volatility and drawdown.
  - Check whether key drivers and risks materialized.

- For your overall process:
  - Distribution of forecast errors (over/underestimation).
  - Accuracy by:
    - Asset class, sector, thesis type (value/growth/macro/turnaround).
    - Conviction level.
    - Time horizon.
  - Identify systematic biases (too optimistic, too tight time horizons, etc.).

**D. Learning & adaptation module**

- **Rule-based learning:**
  - Derive simple rules like:
    - “When I predict >25% return in <6 months, I’m usually overconfident.”
    - “My macro-driven theses underperform my company-specific theses.”
    - “I underestimate risk in highly leveraged companies.”

- **Statistical learning:**
  - Models that estimate:
    - Probability of thesis success given its features.
    - Expected return adjustment based on your historical bias.
  - Use these to:
    - Suggest more conservative/realistic expectations.
    - Flag “dangerous” thesis patterns.

- **Process refinement:**
  - Suggest changes to your templates and checklists.
  - Highlight which indicators you should pay more or less attention to.

---

#### 1.5. Interface layer – how you experience the system

**Core views:**

- **Home dashboard**
  - Portfolio snapshot.
  - Active theses and their status.
  - Open alerts and pending reviews.
  - High-level learning insights (e.g. “Last quarter: your growth theses outperformed, macro theses underperformed”).

- **Instrument view**
  - Price chart + technicals.
  - Your theses timeline.
  - External opinions + quality scores.
  - Your trades and outcomes.
  - Current risk/return profile in portfolio context.

- **Thesis workspace**
  - Structured template for new theses.
  - Embedded or linked documents.
  - Expected return/time horizon inputs.
  - Checklist for drivers, risks, catalysts, exit conditions.

- **Review & learning view**
  - List of theses due for review.
  - For each: expectation vs outcome summary.
  - Guided questions:
    - What went right/wrong?
    - Was the logic sound but timing off?
    - Did something unpredictable happen?
  - Aggregated stats and patterns.

- **Analyst/meta-analysis view**
  - For a ticker/sector:
    - Who covers it.
    - What they say.
    - How good they’ve been historically.
  - Consensus vs contrarian views.

Implementation-wise, this could be a **local web app** (e.g. Streamlit/FastAPI) or a combination of **notebook dashboards + a note-taking tool** (Obsidian/Notion) in early stages.

---

#### 1.6. Automation layer – what runs without you

- **Data refresh jobs**
  - Daily: prices, positions.
  - Weekly/monthly: fundamentals, macro indicators.
  - On-demand: external opinions scraping/import.

- **Monitoring & alerts**
  - Price-based triggers (moves, breakouts, volatility).
  - Fundamental events (earnings, dividend changes, downgrades).
  - Thesis-based triggers (horizon reached, catalyst date, risk condition).
  - Portfolio-based triggers (concentration, risk thresholds).

- **Review scheduler**
  - Monthly/quarterly review sessions.
  - Automatic selection of theses to review.
  - Summary of key divergences and learning opportunities.

- **Learning updates**
  - Periodic recalculation of:
    - Forecast error distributions.
    - Source quality scores.
    - Rule-based insights and model parameters.

### 2. Step-by-step plan – how to build it without drowning

Now, a phased roadmap. Each phase is a usable system on its own, not just scaffolding.

---

#### Phase 0 – Define standards and templates (no code yet)

**Goal:** Make your thinking structured before making the system smart.

- **Design a thesis template** with:
  - Expected return range.
  - Time horizon.
  - Key drivers, risks, catalysts.
  - Valuation logic.
  - Conviction and confidence.
- **Define a minimal taxonomy:**
  - Asset classes, sectors, thesis types, risk buckets.
- **Organize existing files** into a simple folder structure:
  - `/instruments/<ticker>/`
  - `/theses/`
  - `/external/`
  - `/reviews/`

This alone already improves clarity.

---

#### Phase 1 – Minimal data backbone (MVP database)

**Goal:** Get your core entities into a structured form.

- **Implement a simple local database** (SQLite or even CSVs to start) with tables:
  - Instruments
  - Accounts & positions
  - Theses
  - Expectation snapshots
- **Create basic import scripts**:
  - From broker exports (positions, trades).
  - From a simple thesis form (even a CSV/markdown parser).

**Deliverable:** You can query:
- “Show me all theses for ticker X.”
- “Show me all active theses.”
- “Show me my current portfolio with links to theses.”

---

#### Phase 2 – Expectation vs outcome v1 (manual + simple metrics)

**Goal:** Close the loop in a basic way.

- **Add outcome snapshots**:
  - Script to compute actual return since thesis start.
  - Store at review times (e.g. monthly).
- **Create a simple review report**:
  - For each thesis:
    - Expected vs actual return.
    - Time elapsed vs expected horizon.
  - You manually write a short review (why it diverged).

**Deliverable:** A monthly “thesis review” document or dashboard.

This is the first working version of your learning engine.

---

#### Phase 3 – Basic dashboards and UX

**Goal:** Make it pleasant and fast to use.

- **Build a simple local dashboard** (e.g. Streamlit/Jupyter):
  - Portfolio overview.
  - List of active theses with key metrics.
  - Instrument view with:
    - Price chart.
    - Linked theses.
    - Current P&L.
- **Add filters and search**:
  - By ticker, sector, status, conviction, etc.

**Deliverable:** You can sit down, open one interface, and see your world.

---

#### Phase 4 – External opinions & analyst meta-analysis v1

**Goal:** Start capturing and evaluating what others say.

- **Add an ExternalOpinions table**.
- **Create a simple input workflow**:
  - Manual entry or semi-automated parsing of:
    - Source, rating, target, summary.
- **Add basic tracking**:
  - For each source:
    - Track how often they were directionally right.
    - Track average forecast error.

**Deliverable:** For any ticker, you see:
- Who said what.
- Roughly how good they’ve been.

---

#### Phase 5 – Expectation vs outcome v2 (systematic learning)

**Goal:** Turn your history into structured feedback.

- **Compute forecast error metrics**:
  - For each thesis:
    - Error in return.
    - Error in time horizon.
  - Aggregate by:
    - Sector, asset class, thesis type, conviction level.
- **Generate simple insights**:
  - “You tend to overestimate returns in sector X.”
  - “Your 6–12 month horizons often need 12–18 months.”
- **Optionally add simple models**:
  - Regression or classification to predict:
    - Probability of success.
    - Adjusted expected return.

**Deliverable:** A “Learning” view that shows your biases and strengths.

---

#### Phase 6 – Alerts and monitoring

**Goal:** Make the system proactive.

- **Implement scheduled jobs**:
  - Daily price updates.
  - Weekly fundamental updates.
- **Define alert rules**:
  - Price moves beyond thresholds.
  - Thesis horizon reached.
  - Catalyst date approaching.
  - Portfolio concentration breaches.
- **Create an alerts view + notifications**:
  - Even just a daily email or a local “alerts” page.

**Deliverable:** The system taps you on the shoulder when something needs attention.

---

#### Phase 7 – Refinement and optional sophistication

**Goal:** Evolve based on what you actually use.

- **Refine UX**:
  - Remove views you don’t use.
  - Improve the ones you rely on.
- **Deepen analytics where it matters**:
  - If analyst meta-analysis is useful, expand it.
  - If behavioral insights are powerful, add more metrics.
- **Consider light ML**:
  - Only if you feel the marginal value is real, not just “cool”.

---

# 📝 v1 Thesis + Expectation Template

This is the template you’ll use every time you create a new thesis. It’s structured, fast to fill, and designed to support the expectation–outcome loop.

---

## 📌 Thesis Template (v1)

```
# Thesis: <Instrument Name / Ticker>
**Date:**  
**Direction:** long / short / avoid  
**Conviction (1–5):**  
**Confidence in Forecast (1–5):**  

## Summary (2–3 sentences)
<Short description of the idea>

## Core Thesis
- What is the main argument?
- Why does this opportunity exist?
- What is the market missing?

## Valuation Logic
- Current valuation
- Target valuation
- Key metrics (P/E, EV/EBITDA, FCF yield, etc.)
- Relative valuation (sector, history)

## Key Drivers
- <Driver 1>
- <Driver 2>
- <Driver 3>

## Key Risks
- <Risk 1>
- <Risk 2>
- <Risk 3>

## Catalysts
- <Catalyst 1>
- <Catalyst 2>

## What Would Invalidate the Thesis
- <Condition 1>
- <Condition 2>

## Supporting Materials
- Links to files, notes, spreadsheets
```

---

## 🎯 Expectation Snapshot Template (v1)

```
# Expectation Snapshot

## Expected Return
- Bear case: X%
- Base case: Y%
- Bull case: Z%

## Expected Time Horizon
- X–Y months

## Expected Volatility
- low / medium / high

## Expected Drawdown Tolerance
- e.g. -15%

## Expected Path
- smooth / choppy / binary / catalyst-driven

## Notes on Forecast Confidence
- Why do I believe this forecast is reasonable?
- What assumptions am I making?
```

---

## 🔍 Outcome Review Template (v1)

```
# Outcome Review

**Review Date:**  
**Actual Return:**  
**Max Drawdown:**  

## What Happened
- Key events
- Did drivers materialize?
- Did risks materialize?

## Divergence Analysis
- Where did expectation differ from reality?
- Was the logic wrong, timing wrong, or something unpredictable?

## Behavioral Notes
- Did I follow my plan?
- Any emotional deviations?

## Learning
- What rule or insight should I update?
```
